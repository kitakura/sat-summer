Key tips for success

  1. Link your College Board account to Khan Academy on Day 1. This unlocks personalized practice based on your diagnostic score — the platform auto-fills your skill gaps.
  2. Keep an error log. Write down every missed question: what type it was and exactly why you got it wrong. Review it every Sunday. Most improvement comes from this, not more volume.
  3. Always practice with a timer. R&W: ~71 sec/question. Math: ~95 sec/question. Pacing is a skill that needs practice from Week 1, not just before tests.
  4. In Phase 3+, shift from learning to applying. Do full timed modules under real conditions — not just individual practice questions.
  5. The SAT is adaptive. Module 2 gets harder or easier based on Module 1. Accuracy in Module 1 unlocks the harder (higher-scoring) Module 2 — prioritize it.
  6. Use the Bluebook app for at least 2 practice tests. The real SAT is digital — practice in the actual test interface, not just on paper or browser.
  7. Day 60 is a rest day. No studying. Sleep is when memory consolidates. Full rest the night before is worth more than one more practice set.
